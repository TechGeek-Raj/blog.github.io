BEACH WEDDING ATTIRE FOR MEN: OUTFITS & STYLE GUIDE

Photo: iStock

Are you attending a beach wedding this summer? If so, you may wonder what clothes are appropriate for the occasion. Beach wedding attire for men can vary depending on the event’s formality, but there are a few basic guidelines to remember.

First and foremost, consider the type of wedding you will be attending. Is it a destination wedding? A summer wedding? The wedding season and location can play a significant role in what type of attire is appropriate.

For a beach wedding, you’ll want to opt for lighter colors and breathable fabrics to stay cool and comfortable in the sun. It’s also important to consider the formality of the event. Beach weddings can range from casual to formal, so check the dress code before selecting your outfit.

BEACH WEDDING ATTIRE FOR MEN: DRESS CODES

As a guest at a beach wedding, it’s essential to understand the dress code to ensure you look your best while being comfortable in the beach environment. Beach weddings usually have a more laid-back vibe than traditional ones, meaning the dress code may not be as formal. However, it’s still important to dress appropriately for the event’s formality.

Casual Beach Wedding Attire

If the beach wedding dress code is casual, you can choose comfortable and relaxed clothing. This means you can wear a button-up shirt with shorts or khakis. You can also opt for a linen shirt paired with linen pants. Avoid wearing anything too casual, such as flip-flops or tank tops. Instead, opt for boat shoes, white sneakers, or loafers.

Formal Beach Wedding Attire

If the beach wedding dress code is formal, you should dress up more. This means you can wear a suit or a blazer with dress pants. Opt for lighter fabrics such as cotton or linen to stay cool in the beach environment. Avoid wearing black or dark-colored suits, as they can absorb heat and make you feel uncomfortable. Instead, opt for lighter beige, light gray, or blue colors.

Regarding footwear, dress shoes are a must for a formal beach wedding. Avoid wearing sandals or flip-flops, as they are too casual for a formal event. Instead, opt for loafers or dress shoes that are comfortable to walk in.

It’s important to note that some beach weddings may have a specific dress code, such as “beach formal” or “beach chic.” In this case, it’s essential to follow the particular dress code to ensure you fit in with the overall theme of the wedding.

BEST BEACH WEDDING ATTIRE

Photo Credit: Shutterstock

Regarding beach wedding attire for men, it’s essential to balance style and comfort. You want to look sharp and put-together, but you also want to move around and enjoy the festivities without feeling weighed down or overheated. Here are some tips for putting together the perfect beach wedding outfit

Suit Jackets & Blazers

A suit jacket or blazer is a must if you’re attending a formal beach wedding. Opt for lighter colors like beige, light blue, or gray, as these will help keep you cool in the sun. If you feel bold, you can experiment with brighter colors like coral or turquoise. Linen and seersucker suits are also great options for beach weddings, as they’re made from lightweight, breathable fabrics.

Shirts

Photo Credit: Shutterstock

When it comes to shirts, there are a few different options to consider. A dress shirt is always a safe choice, but choose a light fabric like cotton or linen to keep you cool. A polo or linen shirt can also work well if you want something more casual. Just avoid dark colors, as these will absorb more heat and make you uncomfortable.

Pants & Shorts

For pants, a pair of khakis or dress pants can work well. Again, lighter colors are best, but you can experiment with patterns like stripes or checks. If you prefer shorts, cargo shorts or chino shorts are good options. Just make sure to avoid anything too short or tight-fitting.

Shoes

Regarding shoes, boat shoes or loafers are a classic choice for beach weddings. White sneakers or leather sandals can also work well if you prefer something more casual. Just make sure to avoid anything too athletic or bulky-looking.

ACCESSORIES

Finally, don’t forget to accessorize! A Panama hat can add a touch of sophistication to your outfit, while a black bow tie can add a formality. Sunglasses are also necessary for a beach wedding, so bring a pair. Just make sure to avoid anything too flashy or attention-grabbing.

MOST COMMON BEACH WEDDING OUTFITS

You might wonder what to wear when you receive a beach wedding invitation. Beach weddings are more casual than traditional weddings, but you still want to look stylish and appropriate for the occasion. Here are the most common beach wedding outfits for men.

Groom’s Attire

As the groom, you want to look your best on your big day. A beach wedding suit is excellent for a relaxed yet sophisticated look. A linen suit is popular for beach weddings because it is lightweight and breathable. You can also opt for a cotton suit or a blend of linen and cotton.

When it comes to colors, you can’t go wrong with light colors such as beige, light gray, or light blue. You can also choose a suit with a pattern, such as a subtle stripe or check. Pair your suit with a light-colored dress shirt and dress shoes, such as loafers or boat shoes. A tie is optional for a beach wedding.

Wedding Party Attire

The wedding party should complement the groom’s attire. The best bet for the best man and groomsman is to wear the same outfit as the groom but in a different color or pattern. This creates a cohesive look for the wedding party.

You can opt for dress shorts and a dress shirt for a more relaxed look. Dress shorts are an excellent option for a beach wedding because they are lightweight and comfortable

Wedding Guest Attire

As a wedding guest, you want to dress appropriately for the occasion. A suit is not required for a beach wedding but it is still a smart choice. You can opt for a linen or cotton suit in light colors or patterns.

You can wear dress shorts and a dress shirt if you prefer a more casual look. Dress shorts should be knee-length and made of lightweight fabric. Pair your shorts with dress shoes

CHOOSING THE RIGHT BEACH WEDDING ATTIR

When it comes to beach weddings, choosing the proper attire can be a bit tricky. You want to look stylish and appropriate for the occasion while being comfortable in the warm weather. Here are some key factors to consider when selecting your beach wedding attire.

Consider the Formality of the Event

The formality of the wedding will largely dictate what attire is appropriate. If the wedding is labeled “beach formal,” you should choose a dressier look. A dark suit with a white tuxedo shirt or a black tuxedo can create a formal look that is still appropriate for the beach. Consider a lighter-colored suit, such as light blue or beige, for a more casual event. Three-piece suits can also work well for beach weddings.

Consider the Type of Wedding

The type of wedding can also influence your attire choices. If it is a destination wedding, you may want to opt for attire that is easy to pack and doesn’t wrinkle easily. Seaside weddings may call for clothing that is more nautical or beach-inspired. If the wedding is during the summer, you will want to choose lighter fabrics and breathable materials to stay cool.

Consider the Summer Heat

Concerning the summer heat, it’s important to remember this when selecting your beach wedding attire. Lighter fabrics, such as linen or cotton, can help keep you cool while looking stylish. Avoid heavy materials like traditional wool, which can be uncomfortable in warm weather.

Consider the Wedding Invitation

The wedding invitation can provide valuable clues about what type of attire is appropriate. If the invitation specifies a dress code, be sure to follow it. If not, consider the event’s location, time of day, and formality to determine what to wear.

FINAL WORDS

So, as the mercury rises and the wedding season unfolds along the sandy shores, understanding the proper attire for a beach wedding becomes paramount. The key lies in balancing style, comfort, and appropriacy from the groom’s ensemble to the guests’ attire. Always prioritize breathable, light-colored materials like linen or cotton, and don’t be afraid to infuse some beach-inspired flair with tasteful accessories.

Ensure to consider the event’s formality and other details from the invitation to make an informed choice. Ultimately, dressing for a beach wedding isn’t just about surviving the heat—it’s about enhancing the charm of the setting and celebrating the joyous occasion with an effortless style that complements the seaside ambiance. So take a deep breath, soak in the sun, and enjoy the love-filled air in style and comfort at your next beach wedding.
